This project automates the detection and sizing of nanoparticles in Electron Microscopy (SEM/TEM) images. By leveraging computer vision techniques and deep learning, this tool aims to replace manual particle counting‚Äîa bottleneck in materials science research‚Äîwith a robust, automated pipeline.

## üí° Motivation
During my time as a **Project Fellow at STIC** and **Scientific Assistant at VIT**, I operated high-resolution microscopes (JEOL JEM 2100HR, FEI Quanta FEG 250) and manually analyzed over **300 samples per month**. 

The manual characterization of nanomaterials (sizing, distribution analysis) is:
* **Time-intensive:** Hours spent manually measuring particles.
* **Subjective:** Prone to human error and fatigue.

This project bridges my background in **Nanotechnology and Physics** with **Machine Learning** to solve this specific industry challenge.
